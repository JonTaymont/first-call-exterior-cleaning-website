Florida's iron-rich well water and irrigation systems are a leading cause of rust deposits on exterior surfaces. Every time sprinklers run, dissolved iron in the water oxidizes on contact with concrete, stucco, pavers, and pool decks — leaving behind stubborn orange and brown streaks. This is especially common in Cape Coral, where the majority of homes rely on well water for lawn and landscape irrigation.

Our specialized rust treatment process chemically dissolves iron oxide deposits without damaging the underlying surface. We use professional-grade rust removal agents that break down the iron at a molecular level, lifting the discoloration completely. This approach is safe for concrete, pavers, stucco, and natural stone — no aggressive grinding or abrasive blasting required.

We recommend rust treatment every 6–12 months for properties with well water irrigation to keep surfaces looking clean and prevent long-term buildup. Our team can treat driveways, sidewalks, pool decks, walls, and building exteriors — anywhere rust deposits appear. If you're seeing orange or brown streaks around your sprinkler heads, along walkways, or on your pool deck, give us a call for a free estimate.
