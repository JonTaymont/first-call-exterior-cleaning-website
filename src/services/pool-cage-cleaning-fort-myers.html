Pool Cage Cleaning for Fort Myers Properties

If you have a pool in Fort Myers, you have a pool cage. And if you have a pool cage in Fort Myers, it's getting dirty. Algae, mildew, pollen, hard water stains, and general grime coat the screens, frames, and ceiling panels — blocking airflow, darkening your outdoor space, and making the whole area feel neglected.

Fort Myers pool cages face the same challenges as everywhere in SWFL, but properties near the river and canals see accelerated buildup from humidity and waterborne contaminants. Older pool cages with original screens need especially careful treatment to avoid damage.

Our pool cage cleaning process uses calibrated low-pressure soft washing that removes organic growth from aluminum frames and screen panels without bending, stretching, or tearing. We clean the enclosure from top to bottom — ceiling panels, cross-beams, screen mesh, kickplates, and the concrete or paver deck inside.

For Fort Myers homeowners preparing to sell: a clean pool cage is one of the highest-impact, lowest-cost improvements you can make before listing. Buyers notice immediately.

We recommend pool cage cleaning every 12–18 months for most Fort Myers homes. Waterfront properties benefit from more frequent service.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will you damage my pool cage screens during cleaning?▼
No. We use calibrated low-pressure soft washing — not high-pressure blasting. Our technique is safe for standard and fine-mesh screen panels.
How long does pool cage cleaning take?▼
Most standard residential pool cages take 1–2 hours. Larger enclosures or heavily soiled cages may take 2–3 hours.
Can you clean the concrete or pavers inside the pool cage too?▼
Absolutely — we clean the full enclosure including the deck surface. Most customers bundle pool cage cleaning with driveway or house washing for a better price.
