Paver Cleaning & Sealing for Fort Myers Properties

Fort Myers has some of the most beautiful paver work in Southwest Florida — from the brick streets in the River District to the sweeping paver driveways in Gateway, Whiskey Creek, and the neighborhoods along McGregor. But Florida's sun, rain, and humidity are relentless, and unsealed pavers deteriorate fast.

If your paver driveway has started to look faded, if weeds are pushing through the joints, or if you've noticed sand washing out after every rain — it's time for professional cleaning and sealing.

Our process starts with a full power wash to strip away dirt, algae, and old sealant. We re-sand the joints with premium joint sand that locks tight and resists washout. Then we apply a high quality sealer chosen specifically for your paver material and condition — because factors like paver age, material type, and sun exposure all determine which product will give you the best results. Protection lasts 2–3 years against UV fading, staining, weed growth, and ant infiltration.

We've sealed driveways, pool decks, patios, and commercial walkways across Fort Myers. For the River District's brick, we use breathable sealants designed for clay pavers that maintain the historic look while protecting against foot traffic and weather.

Fort Myers HOA communities — we offer multi-property pricing for communities that want consistent curb appeal across all homes. Call us for a neighborhood quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does paver sealing last in Fort Myers?▼
A quality sealant application lasts 2–3 years in Fort Myers conditions. Properties with heavy sun exposure or vehicle traffic may need resealing closer to 2 years. We select the right sealer based on your paver material, age, and exposure.
Can you seal pavers in the River District and downtown Fort Myers?▼
Yes — we use breathable sealants for clay and brick pavers that are appropriate for historic areas. We also seal concrete pavers, travertine, and natural stone.
Do you remove old sealant before resealing?▼
If the existing sealant has failed or is peeling, yes — we strip it completely before applying new sealant. A new coat over failed sealant won't last.
